### Task/assessment brief:

#### Scenario
SkyMatrix Aviation Systems Ltd. is a leading high-tech aviation company that develops and maintains comprehensive aviation ecosystem solutions including critical airport infrastructure systems, air traffic management software, aircraft maintenance tracking systems, and customer-facing digital services. The company operates a cloud-based platform (hosted on Microsoft Azure) that provides integrated services including online flight booking, real-time baggage tracking, personalized travel offers, loyalty program management, and mobile check-in solutions. They handle sensitive passenger data, payment information, flight operations data, aircraft maintenance records, and proprietary aviation algorithms. The company operates from three locations including a main development center and two operational hubs, with 200 employees, and provides systems to over 35 international airports, 12 major airlines, and serves approximately 2.5 million passengers annually through their digital platform.

#### Tasks
Now, as a senior Cyber Security analyst, your responsibility is to train and prepare a presentation to educate these newly trained cyber security officers at Sky Metrix on legal, socio-technical, professional and ethical dimensions of their duty and what they need to adhere to. LO 05)

### Academic or technical terms explained:

**Understanding -**
Student will understand how penetration testers need to work on the legal, socio-technical, professional, and ethical aspects of their duties. We'll cover laws and regulations governing cybersecurity, emphasizing the importance of compliance and proper authorization. Additionally, we'll discuss understanding human behavior, effective communication with stakeholders, ethical responsibilities, and the balance between security testing and patient welfare.

### Aim(s)
This module aims to encourage students to critically reflect on a range of information security concepts and topical issues including information security, cryptography, cryptoanalysis and secure systems. Students will have the opportunity to critically analyse and design practical applications using modern cryptography.

### Learning Outcomes
On successful completion of the module, students should be able to:
* Identify and evaluate information security risks in a variety of real world environments.
* Critically evaluate essential components and principles of cryptography
* Demonstrate an understanding of and justify the use of appropriate security and cryptographic techniques for the design and implementation of secure systems.
* Design and implement symmetric and public-key encryption for real-world applications
* Contextualise the wider legal, socio-technical, professional and ethical dimensions of cyber security.

### Indicative Content
Identification and authentication, confidentially, integrity and availability, access control techniques. Risk management. Security models and evaluation. Physical and environmental security.

An introduction to cryptography: Principles of cryptography; Confidentiality, integrity, non-repudiation, data origin authentication and entity authentication; A history of cryptography; Basic information theory; Essential number theory, algebra and discrete mathematics; Classical, symmetric and asymmetric encryption techniques and algorithms; Steganography; Cryptanalysis;

Encryption and decryption, types of cipher, symmetric and asymmetric key systems, message digests and signatures.

The application of cryptography: An overview of the application of cryptography; Encrypting data at rest; Digital signatures; Digital certificates; Certification authorities; Public-Key Infrastructure (PKI); Securing email and Internet communications (SSL/TLS); Virtual Private Networks (VPNs); The provision for data origin authentication, entity authentication and non-repudiation in network communications; Cryptanalysis and cryptographic backdoors; The future of cryptography.

Cryptaanalysis, steganaography, quantum cryptography
